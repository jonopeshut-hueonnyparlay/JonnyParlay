What to look for

The red line (leading team) should be well above zero at halftime — that means they're shooting above their season average. The green line (trailing team) should be below zero — shooting cold. The wider the gap between the two lines, the stronger the regression signal.

Buy signal

Red line above +5, green line below −3, and area fill between them is wide. This means the leading team's eFG% is 5+ points above their norm — statistically unsustainable on ~15 three-point attempts (binomial SD ±12pp). The trade exists because this gap will compress.

When NOT to trade

If both lines are near zero, the shooting difference is real — not variance-driven. If the red line is only +1–2 above norm, there isn't enough to regress. No gap = no trade.

Second-half read

Watch for the red line dropping toward zero during Q3. That's the regression happening live. As the gap closes, the score compresses and the market will reprice. This is your profit materializing. The line won't activate until 20 FGA to avoid early noise.

Comeback Capacity

Elasticity — can the trailing team generate the scoring bursts needed to close the gap?

A
High capacity = be patient — Dallas has a 71 capacity score. High 3PA rate, fast pace, good turnover creation. They can close a 13-point gap in 3–4 possessions. Hold for full repricing.
B

What to look for

A 0–100 score measuring the trailing team's ability to close gaps quickly. Based on their 3PA rate (heaviest weight), game pace, and turnover creation rate. The dashed line at 50 is league average.

This is an exit timer, not an entry filter

This indicator does NOT trigger or block trades. It tells you how long to hold. High score = the comeback can happen fast, so be patient and let it play out. Low score = it'll be slow, take profits early.

High score ≠ guaranteed comeback

Capacity just means the tools are there. A team can have high 3PA rate and still miss. This indicator calibrates exit timing expectations, not comeback probability. That's what the other indicators are for.

How it changes exit timing

Score 65+: Wait for full repricing — target mid-to-late Q3 exit. Score 40–64: Standard exit — take the first good window. Score below 40: Exit at first sign of profit — don't wait.

Market Edge

The precision tool — live ESX price vs. KairosEdge fair value (Temporal Edge Theory)

+12¢ edge
KairosEdge Fair Value ESX Price (live) Entry Price
A
The edge gap — At halftime, KairosEdge fair value is 46¢ but ESX prices it at 34¢. That's a 12-cent edge. The green shaded area between the lines IS the mispricing — this is what you're trading.
B
Lines converging = exit — As regression happens, the market catches up. When the gold line meets the blue line, the edge is gone. Sell. If gold spikes ABOVE blue, the market overreacted — that's the ideal exit.
What to look for

Two lines: blue = KairosEdge's mathematical fair value (from the Brownian motion formula). Gold dashed = what ESX is actually pricing the contract at. The gap between them = your edge in cents. White dashed = your entry price.

Entry confirmation

At halftime: KairosEdge fair value must be at least 8¢ above ESX price. This 8pp minimum accounts for the favourite-longshot bias documented by Bürgi et al. (2026) — low-price contracts are systematically overpriced, so you need extra margin.

The formula behind the blue line

z = (−deficit + spread × τ) / (12 × √τ), then Fair Value = normalCDF(z) × 100. The spread already includes home court — never add it separately. σ = 12 is NBA scoring standard deviation. τ = remaining time fraction.

This is the exit trigger

Exit when: gold line meets or crosses blue line (edge gone), OR gold spikes above blue during a trailing team run (market overreacted — sell into the spike). Forced exit: 4:00 remaining in Q4 regardless — the Brownian model breaks when teams start fouling and managing clock.

Usage Guide

How to Use the ESX Indicators + KairosEdge

ESX Indicators = the 5 chart panels you watch on ESX during the game.
KairosEdge = the scoring engine that computes the composite score and BUY/PASS decision behind the scenes.

The indicators show you the picture. KairosEdge tells you what to do with it. Here's the full workflow.

PHASE 1
Pre-Game Setup
30–60 minutes before tip-off
1
Open ESX and check tonight's NBA slate

Pull up the ESX indicator panels for tonight's games. You're looking for games where one team is a moderate favorite (spread 2–7 points). Blowout matchups (spread 12+) rarely produce tradeable setups because the better team usually leads for real reasons, not variance.

2
Note the pregame spread for each game

Write down or screenshot the spread. This feeds directly into the Indicator 5 KairosEdge fair value formula. The spread already includes home court advantage — never adjust for HCA separately. You need this number at halftime.

3
Check injury reports

A key player being out changes the team's season averages. If a team's best shooter is out, their "season eFG%" baseline is artificially high — the regression signal will be weaker than it looks. Flag any games with major injuries for reduced confidence.

PHASE 2
First Half — Watch the Indicators Build
Q1 through halftime
4
Watch ESX Indicator 1 (Regression Index) develop

This is the panel you're watching most. As the first half plays out, you'll see the red line (leading team's eFG% deviation) and green line (trailing team's deviation) diverge. You're hoping for a wide gap — red line above +5, green line below −3. The wider the gap, the more the leading team is shooting above their norm. Don't make any decisions yet. Just watch it build.

5
Glance at ESX Indicator 2 (Possession Advantage)

Is the blue line above or below zero? If the trailing team is also losing structurally (paint points, rebounds, turnovers all going the wrong way), the deficit might be real — not just shooting variance. You want to see this line above zero heading into halftime.

6
Let the ESX panels build — don't trade during the first half

This is a halftime system. The ESX indicators are building the picture in real time so you have full context at the break. KairosEdge runs its scoring at halftime, not during live play. No entries until then. The data isn't stable enough until halftime (minimum 20 FGA per team before the regression line even activates).

PHASE 3
Halftime — The Decision
First 1–3 minutes of halftime break
7
Read the ESX panels + check the KairosEdge score — all must pass

This is the moment. Read each ESX indicator panel, then check the KairosEdge composite score:

① ESX Indicator 1: Is the leading team's eFG% deviation above +5? Is the gap between red and green lines wide?
② ESX Indicator 2: Is the composite index above zero? (Trailing team is structurally competitive)
③ ESX Indicator 3: Is the sustainability score below 40? (Leading team's offense is fragile)
④ ESX Indicator 5: Is the blue KairosEdge fair value line at least 8¢ above the gold ESX price line?
⑤ KairosEdge engine: Is the overall composite score ≥ 78/100?
⑥ Hard filters: Deficit under 19 points? Paint differential under 12? Turnovers under 7?

All green? → BUY. Any fail? → PASS. Don't negotiate with the checklist.

8
Place the trade — maker-only limit order

On ESX, find the trailing team's full-game winner market. Place a maker-only limit order (not a taker order — taker fees eat 15–35% of your edge). Your limit price should be at or slightly below the current ask. Entry window is the first 1–3 minutes of halftime — after that, institutional market makers (SIG, DRW, Jump) start repricing and the edge shrinks.

9
Size the position

Use half-Kelly sizing. For a single trade, that's roughly 2.25–3% of bankroll. If you have multiple trades tonight, use quarter-Kelly per trade and cap total exposure at 2× a single position size. NBA game contracts have ~0.73 correlation — don't treat them as independent.

10
Record your entry price

Write down the exact fill price. This becomes the white dashed line on ESX Indicator 5. You need it to calculate your P&L and to track CLV (closing line value) — which is the only true long-term edge validator. If your entry price is consistently better than the closing price, you have a real edge regardless of individual win/loss results.

PHASE 4
Second Half — Monitor and Exit
Q3 through exit
11
Check ESX Indicator 4 (Comeback Capacity) to set your patience level

This is the first thing to check when Q3 starts. It tells you how long to hold:

▸ Score 65+: Be patient. This team has the tools to close fast. Target mid-to-late Q3 for your exit.
▸ Score 40–64: Standard patience. Take the first good window that appears.
▸ Score below 40: Short leash. Exit at the first sign of profit. This team grinds slowly.
12
Watch ESX Indicator 1 — is the regression actually happening?

The red line (leading team's eFG% deviation) should be trending back toward zero during Q3. This is mean reversion in real time — the leading team's shooting is normalizing. As it drops, the score compresses, and the market reprices the trailing team's contract upward. That's your profit building.

13
Mid-Q3: recheck ESX Indicators 2 and 3

Quick gut check — is ESX Indicator 2 still above zero? Is ESX Indicator 3 still below 40? If both are holding, the thesis is intact. Keep holding. If Indicator 2 has dropped below zero (trailing team is now losing structurally too), consider an early exit — the picture has changed.

14
Watch ESX Indicator 5 for the exit signal

This is the exit trigger chart. You're watching two things:

▸ Lines converging: The gold ESX price line is rising toward the blue KairosEdge fair value line. When they meet, the edge is gone. Sell.
▸ Gold spikes above blue: The trailing team goes on a run and the market overreacts — ESX price briefly exceeds KairosEdge fair value. This is the ideal exit. Sell into the spike immediately.

Laddered exit: Sell 50% of your position when the market reaches KairosEdge fair value. Hold the other 50% for further Q3 repricing or a trailing team run.

15
Hard exit: 4:00 remaining in Q4 — no exceptions

The Brownian motion model that powers the KairosEdge fair value calculation breaks down in the final minutes. Teams start fouling intentionally, managing the clock, and playing fundamentally different basketball. The math no longer applies. If you're still holding at the 4:00 mark in Q4, sell everything at whatever price is available. This is a non-negotiable rule.

PHASE 5
Post-Game
After the final buzzer
16
Record the result and calculate CLV

Log: entry price, exit price, profit/loss (after fees), and the closing line value — what the contract was priced at when you exited or at game end. If your entry prices are consistently below the closing line, you have a verified edge. CLV is the single most important metric. Win rate alone means nothing at small sample sizes.

17
Review: did the ESX indicators behave as expected?

Look back at the 5 ESX panels. Did the regression actually happen (Ind 1 red line drop)? Did the structural picture hold (Ind 2 stay positive)? Did the market eventually reprice (Ind 5 lines converge)? This review is how you build intuition over time. After 50+ trades you'll start reading the panels instinctively.

How the 5 ESX Indicators + KairosEdge Work Together

The ESX panels aren't independent — they form a decision chain. KairosEdge reads all 5 and generates the composite score. Here's the read order from tip-off through exit.

1

Check at halftime. Is there a gap? Is the leading team's red line above +5? Is the trailing team's green line below −3? If yes, there's a regression signal. If both lines are near zero, no trade — the shooting difference is real.

2

Validate the signal. Is the blue line above zero? If yes, the trailing team is structurally competitive — they're losing because of shooting, not because they're outmatched. If below zero, suppress the trade. The deficit is real.

Confirm the fragility. Is the leading team's sustainability score below 40? That means their hot shooting is built on unassisted threes and low FT rate — the most fragile profile. If it's above 70, their offense is real. Reduce size or pass.

4

ESX Indicator 5 — Market Edge

Confirm the edge exists. Is the blue KairosEdge fair value line at least 8¢ above the gold ESX price? If yes, the market is underpricing the comeback probability. KairosEdge generates the BUY signal. Place the trade — maker-only limit order, within the first 1–3 minutes of halftime.

5

ESX Indicator 4 — Comeback Capacity → Exit Timing

Calibrate your patience. High capacity (65+) = wait for full repricing, target mid-Q3 exit. Low capacity (below 40) = take the first profitable window. Then watch ESX Indicator 5 — when the lines converge or gold crosses above blue, sell immediately.
